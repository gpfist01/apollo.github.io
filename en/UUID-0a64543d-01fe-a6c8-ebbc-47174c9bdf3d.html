Product voice and tone

TheÂ Voice & Tone section explains how you should write in a general sense. It will explain what the voice & tone should be for:

  • product copy

  • technical documentation

  • help articles

  • non-marketing client documents (e.g., plan PDF)

This does NOT apply for:

  • marketing documents

  • sales documents

  • contracts

  • legal documents

Difference between voice and tone

A company's voice represents the companies personality and values. You can identify a loved one's voice in a crowd the same way you can identify a companies content based on their voice. Our voice must be consistant throughout all the content types identified above.

ToneÂ is akin to someones attitude when speaking. You can say a sentence a certain way and convey that you're agitated or excited. You can do the same when writing. By knowing who you're writing for and what you're writing, you can tailor your word choices and sentences to be motivational, supportive, and more. Your voice must always remain the same but your tone must change depending on the user and the experience.
