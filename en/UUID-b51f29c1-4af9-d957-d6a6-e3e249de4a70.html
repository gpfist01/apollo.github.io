Calls to Action

General Rules

CTA Rule

What it means

Notes

Use title case

Capitalize each letter the way you would a headline. Like headlines, punctuation is case-by-case.

Button: "Get Started or Pay the Price"

Button: "Got Beef?"

Title and CTA should have congruency

Frequently, a button appears as part of a flow, either before or after a header/title. Given this context, it's important for the relationship between the button and title to be clear.

Title: "Are You Sure?"Â 

Button: "Yes, Continue"

On the flip side...

Button: "Connect Account"

Title on the next screen: "Connect Your Bank of America Account"

Use sentence case for secondary CTAs

Secondary CTA (CTAs that aren't a button but rather underlined text) should be sentance case

Adding multiple of the same thing

When you're adding multiple things of a thing (e.g., Add Account, Assign Loan...) no need to say "another"

Button: "Add Account"

Bad Example "Add Another Account"

Continue

When inputting a field is part of a larger process and the flow continues on.

For example, when you're setting up a security question at registration as part of the longer registration, you hit "Continue" to go on to the rest of registration.

Submit

When inputting a field is the final step of a process and ends the flow.

For example, when you're setting up a security question in a standalone security question set up, you'd hit "Submit" the end the flow and go on with your life.

Maybe Later

Hyperlink CTA, often next to a CTA button, that allows the user not to take an action right now and lets them know they can do it later.
